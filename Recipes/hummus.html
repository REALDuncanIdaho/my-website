Hummus

Description

Often I have found myself wanting with the local hummus' that my community has to offer. It tasted so bland, so boring. I thought that's what hummus was, and all it could be. In, short I was an idiot. At the time I could never fully express why, but with what I know now it is clear as day. Tahini. This magically concoction of sesame seeds give hummus the rich flavor it is famous for. The only downside is it is the most expensive ingredient in hummus. This is why the hummus around me leaves much to be desired, they are skimping on the tahini. This is the cardinal sin of hummus, and one I am hopefull to fully avoid.

Note: A word of warning. All measurements are estimates. I tend to cook with my eyes and my heart, and as such, the measurements may not be exact. The big thing to keep in mind is to keep your solids and liquids in equalibrium to get that perfect smooth hummus.

Requirements

  • Food Processor/Blender
  • Instant pot (can cook beans without it but makes it braindead easy)

Ingredients

  • dried chickpeas, 2lbs
  • tahini, 1lbs
  • garlic, as much as you want
  • lemon juice, 2-3 cups
  • water, 1/2 cup
  • ground cumin, 2-3 tbps
  • zaatar spices, 1-1/2 cups
  • red pepper flakes, if wanted that added spice

Steps

  1. Rinse dried chickpeas to remove startches, then place in pot, add water and cook. In instant pot set for 1 hour.
  2. Deskin garlic and add to food processor along with lemon juice. Blend until garlic is chopped. Let sit for 5-10 minutes.
  3. Add tahini to food processor and blend, while blending pause to add water.
  4. Remove chickpeas from pot and rinse once more. Let sit to cool, around 5 minutes.
  5. Add cumin, red pepper flakes, and zaatar spices to processor. Then add chickpeas and blend all together. This step can take some time, go gentle on your processor. Blend into paste. Add water if needed.
  6. Remove, clean up, and enjoy!
